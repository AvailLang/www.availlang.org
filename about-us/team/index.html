The Avail Team

Rich Arriaga

Rich is the Chief Financial Officer of the Avail Foundation, LLC...which is basically a glorified way of saying, "he makes sure we pay our web hosting bill". Rich lingered in college for a bit, and as a result of his extended stay, he holds a bachelors degree in Classical Civilizations from Florida State University, a bachelors degree in Mathematics from the University of South Florida, and a masters degree in mathematics from Cleveland State University. In addition to his experience developing client-facing database applications, he has a diverse professional background that includes data analytics, operations, insurance, and education. Aside from additions to the Avail virtual machine and the Avail standard library, he wrote the very first Avail program; a quiz domain specific language. When not programming, he handles the majority of the business side of the Avail Foundation, LLC.

Spencer George

Spencer is one of the newest members of the Avail team. He holds an Associates of Applied Science as an IT Network Specialist degree, and is currently studying at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee's School of Information Science (SOIS). Currently, Spencer assists with web development.

Skatje Myers

Skatje is a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Colorado at Boulder, under advisement by James Martin. She received a BA in Computer Science from the University of Minnesota-Morris in 2010, and did research in the Biomedical Informatics department at Arizona State University in 2009 through the CRA-W's DREU program. After graduation, she worked as a Java and Smalltalk developer with Mark and Todd.

Leslie Schultz

Leslie joined the Avail team in 2011 and will be graduating from the University of Wisconsin-Madison this May with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. Over the years she's had a variety of job titles, but she always ends up working with and designing software, doing visual design, and writing documentation and training materials. She is currently working on all of the above for the Avail project. Specifically, her ongoing contributions include additions of both code and documentation to the Avail virtual machine and library, writing and editing external documentation, and maintenance of the website.

Todd L. Smith

Todd is the Chief Executive Officer of the Avail Foundation, LLC. He holds a Bachelor of Arts in Classical Civilization from Florida State University. Having learned to read Latin extremely well and Attic Greek fair to middling, he decided to pursue a career in software so that he could actually pay his bills. He has written production-quality software in sixteen general-purpose programming languages for a variety of companies of varying industry, size, and prominence. He has always daydreamed of greater expressivity than was available in traditional programming languages. Avail first asserted its inexorable influence over him when he learned of its existence in 2007 as a result of his new friendship with Mark van Gulik; he was quite unable to resist its seductive promises of articulate programming. As of August 2013, he has written about 1.5 megabytes of Avail source code, including the majority of the Avail standard library, and contributed about 20% of the virtual machine by code volume. When he is poked hard enough, he produces end-user documentation. When he is poked even harder, he focuses on the droll necessities of operating a business that no has near-term commercial prospects.

Mark van Gulik

Mark studied computer languages through the 1980s and early 1990s. In 1993, his dissatisfaction with the state of the art finally led him to start implementing Avail in his spare time. He worked primarily as a consultant in North America and Europe while continuing to develop Avail from 1993 until 2008, when the Avail team started to grow. With this head start, he invented most of the concepts, technologies, and infrastructure of Avail. Mark is currently focusing his effort toward improving portions of the Avail virtual machine such as the dynamic translator and the macro compiler. His training in classical piano somehow survived his collision with computers in high school, although its fate is unclear in the balance of full time work, acting as the Avail Foundation's Chief Technology Officer, actually developing Avail, and raising five kids with his wife in Madison, Wisconsin.
